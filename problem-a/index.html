Michael Corleone

Introduction

Michael Corleone is the main protagonist of Mario Puzo's 1969 novel, The Godfather. Unlike his two older brothers, Michael renounces the Corleone "family business", wanting instead to lead a more Americanized life. He is a person with a strong will. Generally, he is humble, respectful and occasionally shows his domineering. Having received higher education and once worked in the army, He originally wanted to become a high-ranking government official such as governor or senator instead of inheriting the family’s gang business. However, because of the attempted assassination of his father by a hostile force and the killing of his elder brother, he accepted his father’s concession, becoming the godfather.
